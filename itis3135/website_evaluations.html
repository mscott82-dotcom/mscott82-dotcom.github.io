Website Evaluations

The "Good" Website, "juice.agency"

The company behind this website appears to specialize in designing websites.

  • CONTRAST: The page uses good contrast between background elements and text. Not only is it comfortable to read, the color schemes used feel intentional and work well to direct your attention through the page. The color schemes change throughout the page as you scroll, and the respective text and other elements in those sections adapt appropriately as well.
  • REPETITION: The section of the page showcasing the images along with their respective captions all follow a similar design language despite the completely different subject matter depicted in each image. Each image has the same effect of slightly zooming in on mouse hover, large readable captions, and similar spacing rules that work well for Alignment.
  • ALIGNMENT: As you scroll down the page, it alternates the position of images back and forth from the sides of the screen, breaking up potential monotony of having them aligned all one way without being visually distracting.
  • PROXIMITY: There is a lot of open space in this page, most of it used to allow you to focus on only one or two images at a time with little else distracting you on the screen. It feels akin to a well done presentation slideshow adapted to a webpage scrolling experience.

The "Bad" Website - "startrek.htm"

This site was last updated in 2004, and appears to be someones hub for a bunch of star trek related links.

  • CONTRAST: Immediately you'll notice the poor contrast between the text and the background texture. Each block of text uses a different color, and all the links are pure blue on top of a mostly blue/black background.
  • REPETITION: The entire page uses a repeating space texture, that may have once looked cool in 2004, but on modern displays, the tiling effect of an already very noisy image is extremely distracting and makes the text harder to read.
  • ALIGNMENT: Everything on the site is centered in the middle of the screen, which leaves about half of the screen's real estate taken up by the aforementioned tiling space texture.
  • PROXIMITY: Despite all of the extra space on the left and right sides of the page, the content in the middle is squashed together with very little padding to break up paragraphs or blocks of links. This is most evident with the orange colored block of text near the top of the page, which is unpleasant to read due to everything already mentioned and the lack of good spacing.
